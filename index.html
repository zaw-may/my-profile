Slowly Changing Dimension

Slowly Changing Dimensions (SCDs) are a method used in data management to handle changes to data over time. They categorize changes into types, including Type 1 (overwriting old data), Type 2 (maintaining a history of changes), and Type 3 (partially updating while retaining historical data). SCDs are crucial for maintaining data integrity.

RFM Analysis

RFM analysis is a marketing strategy that segments customers based on their transactional behavior. It analyzes three key factors: recency of purchases, frequency of purchases, and monetary value spent. By categorizing customers into segments, businesses can tailor marketing efforts to specific groups for improved engagement and customer relationship management.

Interquartile Range

The interquartile range (IQR) is a measure of statistical dispersion that represents the range between the first quartile (Q1) and the third quartile (Q3) in a dataset. It provides information about the spread or variability of the middle 50% of the data. Calculating the IQR involves finding the difference between Q3 and Q1: IQR = Q3 - Q1.

We say data is useless when it does not provide meaningful insights, value, or relevance to the task at hand or the goals of the organization. Useless data can be irrelevant, outdated, inaccurate, or too voluminous to analyze effectively. In such cases, it can hinder decision-making processes and waste resources.
